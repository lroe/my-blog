-The Controller (C) is the final piece. In this paper, the authors did something very unusual: they made the Controller as small and "dumb" as possible.


Critical Perspective: The "Disconnected" Brain
This is the most controversial part of the paper's architecture.

The Model's Claim: V and M are trained first (unsupervised), and C is trained last.

The Risk: What if the VAE (Vision) decides that "Green Grass" is very important to reconstruct, but it completely ignores the "Grey Road" because the grass takes up more pixels?

If the World Model doesn't "see" or "predict" the road correctly, the Controller can never learn to drive, no matter how much you evolve it. The Controller is "trapped" by whatever the World Model decides is important. In biological brains, our goals actually change how we perceive the world (e.g., when you're hungry, you notice food signs more). This AI can't do that; its "eyes" are fixed.
