Chapter 9: Measuring Circuits

The Observer Effect

In physics, you can rarely measure something without changing it. To measure current or voltage, you must become part of the circuit.

Instruction: Grab the probes and touch them anywhere on the silver wires to begin measuring!

The Voltmeter

A Voltmeter measures potential difference between two points. It must be connected in parallel.

Quest: Place the red and black probes on different parts of the circuit to see the voltage between them.

The Ammeter

An Ammeter measures flow. To see all the electrons, it must be placed in series.

Quest: Switch to AMPS mode and place both probes close together on the SAME wire segment to measure the current passing through that point.

Conclusion

An ideal voltmeter should never steal current. Use the buttons below to switch between Ideal and Cheap quality.

Notice how a high-resistance circuit's voltage drops when a "cheap" voltmeter is attached. It provides a new path for current!
