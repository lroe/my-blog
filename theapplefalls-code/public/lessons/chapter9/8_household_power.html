Chapter 9: Household Power & Energy

Behind the Walls

Every appliance in your home is part of a massive parallel circuit connected to the city's power grid.

Quest: Drag appliances from the right and "plug" them into the wall outlet. Watch the total current increase as you add more load.

The Breaker Limit

Standard household outlets are limited by a Circuit Breaker. If you try to pull too much current through one wire, it will melt!

Warning: Most home breakers trip at 15 Amps. Try plugging in both the Heater and the Kettle at once.

Wattage vs. Current

Appliances are labeled by Power (Watts), not Current. In the US, the supply is a steady 120 Volts.

\(I = \frac{P}{V}\)

A 1500W heater pulls \(1500 / 120 = 12.5\) Amps. That's almost the entire capacity of one circuit!

Conclusion

You don't pay for Current; you pay for Energy. Energy is Power multiplied by Time.

\(Energy (kWh) = \frac{Watts \times Hours}{1000}\)

Check the "Electric Bill" meter. It shows how much you're spending per hour at a rate of $0.15 per kWh.
