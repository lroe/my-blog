Chapter 8: The Limits of Ohm's Law
Conclusion

Is Resistance always a constant? In Lesson 3, we derived conductivity from collisions. But there's a catch: when a material gets hot, its atoms vibrate more violently.

Higher Temperature $\implies$ More Jitter $\implies$ More Collisions $\implies$ Higher Resistance.

This is why high-power devices like light bulbs are Non-Ohmic. As the voltage goes up, the resistance doesn't stay still—it climbs.
